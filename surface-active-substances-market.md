<p><p>The surface-active substances market is categorized into several types based on their ionic charge properties. Anionic surfactants, which carry a negative charge, are commonly used in detergents and personal care products for their excellent cleaning properties. Non-ionic surfactants, with no charge, are preferred for their mildness and versatility in formulations. Cationic surfactants, positively charged, are often used in conditioners and antimicrobial applications. Amphoteric surfactants can act as both anions and cations, adapting to various environments. Additionally, other surfactants include specialty formulations catering to niche applications.</p></p>
