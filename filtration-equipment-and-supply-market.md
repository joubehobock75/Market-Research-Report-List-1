<p><p>Filtration equipment and supply markets encompass various types designed for specific applications. Microfiltration employs porous membranes to remove larger particles, while ultrafiltration targets smaller contaminants like bacteria and viruses. Reverse osmosis offers high purification by using semi-permeable membranes to eliminate dissolved solids. Nanofiltration, situated between reverse osmosis and ultrafiltration, selectively removes divalent ions and larger organic molecules. Other filtration methods include gravity and sand filters, each catering to unique filtration needs across industries, ranging from water treatment to pharmaceuticals.</p></p>
