<p><p>The biodegradable bags market includes several key types: </p><p>1. **Poly Lactic Acid (PLA)** - Derived from renewable resources like cornstarch, PLA bags are compostable and offer a good balance of strength and biodegradability. </p><p>   </p><p>2. **Starch-Based** - These bags are made from plant starch and designed to decompose quickly, making them suitable for various applications. </p><p>3. **Polyhydroxyalkanoates (PHA)** - Produced by microbial fermentation of sugars or lipids, PHA bags are fully biodegradable in various environments.</p><p>4. **Polybutylene Succinate (PBS)** - PBS offers durability and biodegradability, suitable for food packaging.</p><p>5. **Cellulose-Based** - Sourced from plant cellulose, these bags are biodegradable, compostable, and often used in food applications. </p><p>6. **Others** - This category includes innovative materials and blends designed for specific biodegradable applications.</p></p>
